The Point Photos feature lets you attach photographs to individual survey points. Photos are automatically compressed for efficiency and included in the generated PDF reports, providing visual documentation alongside your coordinate data.

<Note>
  Used in the **Signaletic Sheet**, **Axis Control Sheet**, and **Control Sheet** tools.
</Note>

## What is this feature?

On site, it is common practice to photograph the monumentation of each survey point -- a nail, a stake, a painted mark, a benchmark. These photos serve as proof of what was measured and help resolve ambiguities later. Topo.ninja lets you attach these photos directly to the points in your project.

## How to use it

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open a point's detail view">
    In any tool that supports photos (Control Sheet, Axis Control Sheet, Signaletic Sheet), click on a point to open its detail view or expand its photo section.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Upload a photo">
    Click the photo upload area or drag and drop an image file. You can also take a photo directly from your phone or tablet camera if you are using Topo.ninja in the field.

    Supported formats: **JPEG**, **PNG**, **WebP**.

    <Info>
      You can attach multiple photos to a single point. This is useful when you need to show the point from different angles or document the surrounding context.
    </Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Automatic compression">
    Uploaded photos are automatically compressed to keep file sizes manageable:
    - **Maximum file size**: 1 MB per photo
    - **Maximum resolution**: 1024 px on the longest side

    This compression happens in your browser before the photo is stored. The quality remains sufficient for visual identification in the PDF.

    <Tip>
      You do not need to resize photos before uploading. Even high-resolution photos from modern smartphones are compressed automatically.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review in the PDF">
    When you generate the PDF, each point's photos appear alongside its coordinates, notes, and deviation data (if applicable). Photos are laid out to fit the page without excessive size.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Features

### Multiple photos per point

Attach as many photos as needed to a single point. Common use cases:
- Close-up of the monumentation (nail, marker)
- Context view showing the surroundings
- Before/after comparisons

### Auto-compression

All photos are compressed automatically in your browser:
- No manual resizing needed
- Keeps PDF file sizes reasonable
- Quality remains sufficient for field documentation

### Included in PDF

Photos are embedded in the generated PDF document. They appear in the point detail section, alongside coordinates and notes. This creates a self-contained report that does not depend on external files.

### Privacy

Photos are processed entirely in your browser. They are not uploaded to any server unless you explicitly save the project to cloud storage. Your field documentation stays private.

## Tips

<Tip>
  **Take photos before measuring**: photograph the point monumentation before measuring it. This way, if there is any question about which mark was measured, you have visual proof.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Include context**: in addition to the close-up of the marker, take a wider shot showing the surroundings. This helps identify the point location when reviewing the report later.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **File format**: JPEG is the most efficient format for photographs. PNG works but produces larger files. Avoid raw formats -- they will be converted and compressed anyway.
</Tip>
