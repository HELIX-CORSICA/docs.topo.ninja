The **Signaletic Sheet Generator** is the most feature-rich tool in Topo.ninja. It lets you compare your design points (project) with your field-measured points (implanted), compute deviations, attach photos and notes, and produce a comprehensive PDF report.

<Note>
  **Available on:** Standard, Premium, Enterprise
</Note>

## What is this tool?

After a stakeout, you go back to the field to check what was actually built. You measure the as-built positions and need to compare them against the original design. The Signaletic Sheet Generator handles the full workflow:

- Import or enter both project and implanted points
- Classify points by role (Project vs. Implanted)
- Automatically pair each implanted point with its project counterpart
- Compute 2D and 3D deviations
- Attach photos and notes
- Generate a detailed PDF report

The result is a clear, professional document showing point-by-point how the field matches the design.

## How to use it

<Steps>
  <Step title="Import your points">
    Start by importing or entering your points. You can:
    - Import a CSV file containing both project and implanted points
    - Enter points manually
    - Import two separate CSV files (one for project, one for implanted)

    <Tip>
      If your CSV contains both sets of points, use the Role Assignment feature in the next step to classify them.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Assign roles">
    Each point needs a role: **Project** (design) or **Implanted** (field-measured). The Role Assignment interface provides a three-column drag-and-drop layout:
    - **Center Pool**: unassigned points
    - **Project column**: design points
    - **Implanted column**: field-measured points

    The tool can auto-detect roles based on naming conventions (e.g., points containing "stkd" or "imp" tokens are automatically classified as implanted).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Pair points">
    Once roles are assigned, pair each implanted point with its corresponding project point. Two pairing methods are available:

    **Name-based pairing**: extracts common tokens from point names and matches them. For example, "P1_project" and "P1_implanted" will be paired based on the shared "P1" token.

    **Distance-based pairing**: finds the nearest project point within a configurable threshold (2D or 3D distance). Useful when naming conventions are inconsistent.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add photos and notes">
    For each point or pair, you can:
    - Attach one or more photos (auto-compressed for efficiency)
    - Add a text note (field observation, condition of the marker, etc.)

    Photos and notes are included in the final PDF alongside the deviation data.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review deviations">
    The tool computes and displays:
    - **dX, dY, dZ**: differences per axis
    - **2D distance**: horizontal deviation
    - **3D distance**: total spatial deviation

    Use the bulk editor (Premium/Enterprise) for a spreadsheet-like view with sorting and filtering.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Generate the PDF">
    Click **Generate PDF** to produce a report containing:
    - Project header information
    - Point-by-point comparison table with deviations
    - Photos and notes per point
    - Summary statistics

    The PDF is generated in your browser and downloaded automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Features

### Role assignment

The three-column drag-and-drop interface makes it easy to classify large batches of points. Auto-detection based on naming tokens (e.g., "stkd", "imp", "project") speeds up the process. Multi-select, lasso selection, and keyboard shortcuts are supported.

### Automatic pairing

Two methods ensure you can pair points regardless of naming conventions:
- **Name-based**: fast and reliable when names follow a pattern
- **Distance-based**: works with any naming, matches by proximity

### Bulk editor

On Premium and Enterprise plans, the bulk editor provides a full spreadsheet experience with copy-paste from Excel, fill handle, sorting, filtering, and context menus.

### Photos and notes

Attach field photos and observations to any point. Photos are auto-compressed (max 1 MB / 1024 px) to keep file sizes manageable while maintaining quality for the PDF.

### CSV import/export

Import your points from any CSV format. Export the comparison results including deviations for use in other software.
