The **Control Sheet** is the foundational tool for generating field reports. You enter your survey points with their coordinates, add notes or photos, and generate a formatted PDF ready to print or share.

<Note>
  **Available on:** All plans
</Note>

## What is this tool?

During a stakeout control on site, you measure points and need to produce a summary document. The Control Sheet lets you:

- Gather all project information (client, site, operator, reference, date)
- List your points with their X, Y, Z coordinates
- Add observations or photos per point
- Visualize the points on an interactive map
- Generate a professional PDF in one click

This is the document you hand over to the project owner, site manager, or keep in your project file.

## How to use it

<Steps>
  <Step title="Fill in the project information">
    Start by completing the project form:
    - **Client**: the name of the project owner or client
    - **Site**: the name or address of the job site
    - **Operator**: your name or the surveyor's name
    - **Reference**: the file or order number
    - **Date**: the date of the survey

    This information will appear in the PDF header.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add your points">
    You have two options for adding points:

    **Manual entry**: click the add button and enter the point name along with its X, Y, and Z coordinates.

    **CSV import**: import a CSV file containing your points. The expected format is straightforward: one column per field (name, X, Y, Z). The tool detects columns automatically.

    <Tip>
      If you have many points, CSV import will save you considerable time. You can export from your field book or post-processing software.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enrich your points (optional)">
    For each point, you can:
    - Add a **note** (field observation, remark about the monumentation, etc.)
    - Attach a **photo** (taken with your phone or tablet)

    Notes and photos will appear in the PDF next to the corresponding point's coordinates.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Check the map preview">
    The map preview displays all your points on a basemap. Use it to visually verify that your points are consistent and correctly positioned.

    <Warning>
      If your points do not appear on the map, check that the coordinates are in a recognized projected system (Lambert 93, UTM, etc.) and not in a local system without georeferencing.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Generate the PDF">
    Click **Generate PDF**. The document is created in your browser and downloads automatically. It contains:
    - The header with project information
    - The full table of points and coordinates
    - Notes and photos
    - A map preview
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Features

### Project form

The project form holds the metadata for your survey. All fields are optional, but filling in at least the client and site makes your document more professional and easier to find later.

### CSV import and export

You can import your points from a CSV file and export them at any time. This is handy for:
- Pulling in data from your total station or GNSS receiver
- Saving your work to resume later
- Sharing data with a colleague

### Point photos

Each point can have one or more photos. On site, photograph the monumentation (nail, stake, marker) directly from your phone or tablet. The photo will be included in the final PDF.

### Map preview

The interactive map shows all your points at a glance. You can zoom, pan, and verify the consistency of your survey before generating the PDF.
